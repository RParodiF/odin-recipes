Steak Tartare

Description

Steak tartare is a dish made of finely chopped raw beef, typically served with onions, capers, and seasonings such as mustard, Worcestershire sauce, and hot sauce. It is often garnished with a raw egg yolk on top and served with toast points or crackers. The dish is traditionally made with high-quality, tender cuts of beef such as filet mignon or sirloin. The beef is finely chopped by hand or with a sharp knife, and then mixed with the other ingredients. Steak tartare is a popular dish in many countries, including France, Belgium, and Germany, and it has become a popular menu item at restaurants around the world. It is typically served as an appetizer or as a main course, and it is often considered a delicacy due to the high-quality beef used in its preparation.

Make sure to use high-quality, fresh beef when making this dish, as raw beef can carry bacteria that can cause foodborne illness. Make sure to handle the beef carefully and store it properly to minimize the risk of contamination.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound high-quality, tender beef such as filet mignon or sirloin, trimmed of any fat or sinew
  • 2 tbsp finely chopped onion
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • Hot sauce, to taste
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 egg yolk (optional, but recommended)

Steps

  1. Cut the beef into small, evenly sized cubes.
  2. Finely chop the beef by hand or with a shark knife, until it is in very small pieces. Be sure to chop the beef as finely as possible, as this will ensure a smooth texture
  3. Place the chopped beef in a bowl and add the onion, capers, mustard, Worcestershire sauce, and hot sauce. Mix the ingredients well
  4. Season the mixture with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. If desired, top the steak tartare with a raw egg yolk.
  6. Serve the steak tartare immediately, with chips or salad.
